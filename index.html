Why three components
Three dimensions, three different stories
Each variable is measured independently and tells you something the others cannot. Knowing a builder is large doesn't tell you how fast they sell. Knowing they sell fast doesn't tell you if they actually close. All three together give a complete pre-screen picture.
Scale doesn't tell you efficiency. A builder with 500 homes in their pipeline could be selling fast or could have years of unsold inventory sitting still.
Efficiency doesn't tell you risk. A fast-selling builder could still be starting more homes than they close, creating a gap between construction activity and revenue.
Risk is most independent. r=0.20 with Efficiency — the weakest correlation of any pair. A builder can score well on both Scale and Efficiency and still have execution risk.
Overlap labels show correlation. Lower r = more independent information. All three are below 0.60 — each genuinely adds signal beyond the others.
01
Component 01 — Scale
How large is the builder's live pipeline?
Primary variable
Active Construction Inventory
UC units + finished vacant + model homes
Every home currently being built or sitting ready to sell. Captures the builder's live operational footprint — not historical volume, not future plans. A builder with 500 homes in their pipeline is a materially different credit than one with 5.

Preferred over Total Portfolio (includes all-time history) and Annual Starts (only 13,600 builders).

02
Component 02 — Efficiency
How fast does inventory convert to revenue?
Primary variable
Months of Supply
Active inventory ÷ monthly sales pace (pre-computed by Zonda)
How many months it would take to sell all active inventory at the current sales pace. A builder clearing inventory in 6 months is operationally lean. One with 5 years of supply has a structural demand problem — regardless of pipeline size.

8× more discriminative spread across the builder population than any alternative efficiency variable.

03
Component 03 — Financial Risk
Are starts actually reaching closing?
Primary variable
Completion Rate
Annual closings ÷ annual starts
The fraction of homes begun that reach closing. A rate at or above 1.0 means the builder delivers on commitments. Below 1.0 flags a gap between construction activity and revenue — with direct implications for repayment capacity.

Most independent from Efficiency (r=0.20) — finds risk that fast sell-through alone doesn't reveal. Where price data is available, Finished % of Asset Value adds balance sheet composition context.
